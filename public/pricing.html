How is Dragoon so cheap compared to its competitors?

Ok, in an unusually transparent manner, we going to share how we create such a low-cost service. The TLDR summary is that we are not a large company with lots of mouths to feed. We've concentrated on keeping our costs low and then sharing those savings with our customers:

  • Subscription income
  • Infrastructure Costs
  • Profitability

The price assumes that all paying customers will subscribe for a period of 12-months. We don't enforce this period in a contract, but rely on a great customer experience and delivering exactly what we promise to retain everyone for as long as possible.

  • £120 p/a in for each subscription
  • £2,500 p/a out for cloud hosting fees & 3rd-party services
  • 21 concurrent subscribers required in order to break even (excluding labour costs)
  • 100 licenses minimum buy-in for channel partners (£12,000 p/a)
  • 10 channel-partners required in order to for profit to reach £120,000 p/a to pay for a small support team

Most software is provided from the open-source community with integration done by the in-house development team who have agreed not to take a salary until profits allow for it (rewarded/supplimented by equity in the business).

Hosting infrastructure makes use of Cloud (pay-per-use) services, to keep costs low whilst maintaining high security standards and accreditaition of the data centre. It is this ad-hoc access to infrastructure which ultimately makes our pricing structure feasible.

Equally, the IT security market is saturated and therefore highly competitive, meaning there is always a deal to be done - savings made on 3rd-party services are shared with all customers.

  • £2,500 p/a for cloud hosting fees & 3rd-party services
  • 44% profit margin with 100 users, growing to 49% margin with 500 users (then plateus out)

The geniune driver for Dragoon to exist is to ensure that security services are affordable and accessible (i.e. reducing technical skill required to use) to all businesses no matter how big or small. The average charge from SECaaS vendors is around £800 pcm which is only feasible to the large, elite few. We believe this is unacceptable.

Having said that, all businesses need to be profitable otherwise they are not viable. Dragoon has found a way to satisfy both affordability and a viable business case by selling high numbers of licenses with a horizontal scaling model for infrastructure which plateaus out, meaning higher profits with larger numbers of subscribers. Essentially:

  • For every 500 concurrent subscriptions, we make a profit of £57,485 p/a (excluding salaries)
  • With 10,000 concurrent subscriptions (an achievable feit), profit would be almost £1million p/a

1st of its kind, Dragoon is a Security as a Service (SECaaS) affordable and ideal for small companies and start-ups.

Forget about security, outsource it (just like you outsource payroll to an accountant) and concentrate on your business.
